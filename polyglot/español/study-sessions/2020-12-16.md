### Review "Like" in Spanish
* _A Eduardo le gusta_ means _Eduardo likes
* _Nos gusta_ means _we like_
* _les gusta_ means _they like_ (multiple people) 
* _Les gusta ir al parque._  They like going to the park. 
* _a mi me gusta_
* _a ellos les gusta_
* A mi me gusta ir de compras.  I like shopping. 
* A ti te gusta correr en el parque?  Do you like running in the park?
* The "A" before the "like" statement in Spanish: sometimes this is used to emphasize who it is who likes something. But sometimes it’s used just because!

### Me and Nos 
* In English the terms "me" or "us" come after the verb.  Example:  You know _me_ or They know _us_. 
* In Spanish, such words come before the verb.  Example: Tu me ayudas.  You help me.  Mis padres siempre nos ayudan.  My parents always help us. 
* Dolingo nos ayuda aprender espanol. 

### Use of "Me quieres" vs. "Me amas" 
* _Te Quiero_ and _te amo_ both mean _I love you_ in Spanish.
* Generally _te quiero_ is used in casual moments with close friends or extended family members. 
* _Te Amo_ is generally reserved for romantic partners or immediate family members and is used in more serious situations. 
* Examples:  Adiós, ¡te quiero!  (Goodbye, I love you!) 
  ¡Te amo! (I love you!)

### Tarde
Tarde can be translated Afternoon or Evening.  But it can also mean late.  
Examples:
*  Mi Madres siempre trabaja en la tarde.  <br>
   My mother always works in the afternoon. <br>
*  Siempre me levanto tarde en la mañana.<br>
   I always get up late in the morning.<br>
*  Mis padres siempre comen tarde en la tarde.<br>
   My parents always eat late in the evening.<br>

# Stem Changes 
## Let’s talk about Stem Changing Verbs!
* Some verbs  use the regular endings, but have a vowel
 change (except in the nosotros  form). 
* For example, o changes to ue, or e changes to ie.
* Irregular verb examples: 

		PODER (to be able to) 
		yo puedo 
		tu puedes
		el/ella/usted puede 
		nosotros/nosotras podemos 
		ellos/ellas/usteds pueden 
		
		PERDER (to lose) 
		yo pierdo 
		tu pierdes
		el ella usted pierde
		nosotros nosotras perdemos 
		ellos ellas ustedes pierden
